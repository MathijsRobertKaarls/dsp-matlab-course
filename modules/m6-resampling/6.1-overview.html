6.1 — Resampling Overview

Module 6 · Resampling · ~15 min

Introduction

Different systems operate at different sampling rates. A microphone might capture at 48 kHz; a codec transmits at 8 kHz; a storage system archives at 44.1 kHz. To move signals between these worlds, you need resampling — changing the sampling rate of a digital signal while preserving its content.

Resampling also appears in frequency shifting, image scaling, and synchronization. Module 6 covers all the key operations: upsampling, downsampling, zero stuffing, and rational-factor resampling.

Core Concept

Why Resampling is Tricky

You might think: "just pick samples at the new rate." But there are two fundamental challenges:

  1. Upsampling: you need new sample values that don't exist yet — requiring interpolation.
  2. Downsampling: you need to throw away samples — but if the signal has high-frequency content, discarding samples causes aliasing. So you must lowpass filter first.

Rational-Factor Resampling

To change the sampling rate by a rational factor $P/Q$:

  1. Upsample by P: insert $P-1$ zeros between each sample.
  2. Lowpass filter: smooth out the zeros and prevent aliasing.
  3. Downsample by Q: keep every $Q$th sample.
$$f_{s,new} = \frac{P}{Q} \cdot f_{s,old}$$
Example: Converting from 44.1 kHz (CD) to 48 kHz (DAT): $P/Q = 48000/44100 = 160/147$. Upsample by 160, filter, downsample by 147. In practice done efficiently with polyphase filterbanks.

Mathematical Foundation

Sampling rate conversion by $P/Q$ can be represented as an ideal interpolation followed by resampling:

$$y[m] = x_a(m \cdot T_{s,new}) = x_a\!\left(\frac{m Q}{P f_s}\right)$$

where $x_a(t)$ is the continuous (bandlimited) signal reconstructed from $x[n]$. In practice, this ideal reconstruction is approximated by a polyphase filter.

Conceptual Questions

Q1. Converting from 44.1 kHz to 48 kHz requires what resampling factor?

  • Upsample by 48, downsample by 44
  • Upsample by 160, downsample by 147 (ratio 160/147)
  • Downsample only by 44.1/48
  • Simply change the sampling rate label without processing

Q2. Why is a lowpass filter needed in the resampling chain?

  • To amplify the signal to unity gain
  • To remove spectral images after upsampling and prevent aliasing before downsampling
  • To synchronize the two sample clocks
  • To reduce quantization noise

Q3. Polyphase filterbanks are used for resampling because they:

  • Produce better audio quality than direct resampling
  • Compute the rational-rate conversion efficiently without explicitly generating all intermediate upsampled samples
  • Eliminate the need for a lowpass filter
  • Allow non-integer resampling ratios exactly

MATLAB Exercise

%% 6.1 Resampling Overview — resample() demo

%% Generate test signal
fs_orig = 8000;
T = 1;
t_orig = (0:1/fs_orig:T-1/fs_orig);
f0 = 440;
x = sin(2*pi*f0*t_orig) + 0.3*sin(2*pi*880*t_orig);

%% Resample to different rates using MATLAB's resample()
% resample(x, P, Q) converts from fs_orig to (P/Q)*fs_orig
rates = [4000, 16000, 22050, 44100];
P_Q = {[1 2], [2 1], [441 160], [441 80]};   % approximate ratios

figure('Name','Resampling Demo');
subplot(length(rates)+1, 1, 1);
plot(t_orig, x); title(sprintf('Original: fs=%d Hz', fs_orig)); xlabel('Time (s)');

for i = 1:length(rates)
  [P, Q] = deal(P_Q{i}(1), P_Q{i}(2));
  y = resample(x, P, Q);
  fs_new = fs_orig * P / Q;
  t_new  = (0:length(y)-1) / fs_new;
  subplot(length(rates)+1, 1, i+1);
  plot(t_new, y);
  title(sprintf('Resampled: fs=%d Hz (P=%d, Q=%d)', round(fs_new), P, Q));
  xlabel('Time (s)');
end

%% Verify: spectrum should show same frequencies regardless of rate
figure('Name','Spectrum Before/After Resampling');
[P2, Q2] = deal(2, 1);
y2 = resample(x, P2, Q2);
fs2 = fs_orig * P2 / Q2;

N1 = length(x);  N2 = length(y2);
subplot(2,1,1);
stem((0:N1/2-1)*fs_orig/N1, abs(fft(x))*2/N1, 'filled', 'MarkerSize',3);
title('Original spectrum'); xlabel('Hz'); xlim([0 1200]);
subplot(2,1,2);
stem((0:N2/2-1)*fs2/N2, abs(fft(y2))*2/N2, 'filled', 'MarkerSize',3);
title('After 2× upsampling (same frequencies, higher Nyquist)'); xlabel('Hz'); xlim([0 1200]);
💡 Hint

After resampling, the signal should still contain exactly 440 Hz and 880 Hz — just represented at a different sample rate. The spectrum plot confirms this: both peaks appear at the same frequencies regardless of the sampling rate.

Key Takeaways

  • Resampling changes the sampling rate of a digital signal while preserving content.
  • Rational-factor: upsample by $P$ → lowpass filter → downsample by $Q$.
  • The LPF prevents both spectral images (from upsampling) and aliasing (before downsampling).
  • In MATLAB: resample(x, P, Q) handles the complete resampling chain efficiently.
